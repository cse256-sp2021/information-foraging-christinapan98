Curriculum Overview

The curriculum at the Washington University Nursery School is designed to enhance your child’s development in four key areas: Social/Emotional Development, Intellectual Development, Physical Development and the Visual Arts.

Extra assistance is also provided for any child who may require help adjusting to the school environment at WUNS.

A full explanation of Washington University Nursery School’s philosophy and educational practices may be found in the Curriculum Overview.


Social/Emotional Development

Individual children's capabilities are valued and strengthened within the group throughout the curriculum.

Intellectual Development

Children's intellectual growth are promoted in four areas: language, math, science and social studies. Here is a quick overview of the ways in which each area is targeted.

    Language: Verbally communicating and listening to others, dramatic play, listening to and telling stories

    Math: Identifying shapes, sizes, patterns, and sequences, sorting and categorizing into sets, counting and comparison, one-to-one correspondence

    Science: Observing seasonal changes related to weather, plant life, animal behavior and activities of people/families, sorting and categorizing items according to various properties

    Social Studies: Learning about similarities and differences in peers and their families, inviting family members to visit school and share items or stories pertaining to their history

Physical Development

Here is an overview of the tools that are used to develop a child's gross and fine motor skills.

Gross Motor: Climbers, slides, trikes, scooters, swings, balls to kick, throw and bounce, bean bags to toss, bubbles to chase

Fine Motor: Tracing, copying, writing letters of the alphabet, writing in journals, ssing clipboards for portable writing or drawing desks in the classroom, on the playground and during field trips

Visual Arts

At Washington University Nursery School we offer children many opportunities to express themselves creatively and artistically. We value visual arts, music, and dramatic play as important building blocks in constructing learning. For a full explanation, please view the curriculum overview link.

Extra Help

If one or more children need help in a specific area, the curriculum may be adapted in a variety of ways, which may include: Offering more one-on-one teacher-student time to understand the child’s thinking, process, build trust, and increase student confidence, offering more opportunities for exploration and more. For a full explanation, please view the curriculum overview link.
